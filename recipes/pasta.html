Home Made Pasta

This is a recipe for handmade fresh Ratini pasta.

After this youll be equiped with all the tools, knowlege, and technique to develop deliciuse pasta within minutes no matter the shape or size.

    Ingredients

  • All-Purposr Flour (2 cups)
  • Eggs (2)
  • Olive Oil (1 Tbs)
  • Salt (1/2 Ts)
  • Water (optional if dry)

    Instructions

  1. Prepare the Flour: On a clean work surface, make a mound with the flour. Create a well in the center of the mound.
  2. Add Eggs: Crack the eggs into the well. If you’re using olive oil and salt, add them to the well with the eggs.
  3. Mix the Dough: Using a fork, gently beat the eggs and gradually start incorporating the flour from the edges of the well. Continue until the dough starts to come together.
  4. Knead the Dough: Once the mixture is too thick to mix with a fork, use your hands to knead the dough. Knead until the dough is smooth and elastic, about 10 minutes. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour. If it’s too dry, add a small amount of water, a teaspoon at a time.
  5. Rest the Dough: Wrap the dough in plastic wrap or cover it with a damp cloth and let it rest at room temperature for at least 30 minutes. This relaxes the gluten and makes it easier to roll out.
  6. Roll and Cut the Dough: After resting, divide the dough into smaller portions. Roll each portion out on a lightly floured surface to about 1/8 inch thick using a rolling pin.
  7. Cut into Strips: Cut the rolled-out dough into thin strips about 1/4 inch wide and 4-5 inches long.
  8. SKIP TO Shaping the Rotini BEFOR THIS Dry the Pasta: Let the shaped pasta dry for about 30 minutes to an hour before cooking.
  9. Cook the Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the rotini and cook for 2-4 minutes or until al dente (fresh pasta cooks much quicker than dried pasta). Drain and serve.
    Shaping the Rotini
  1. Take each strip and wrap it around a thin rod or skewer, creating a spiral shape.
  2. Carefully slide the formed rotini off the rod or skewer and place them on a lightly floured surface or a drying rack.
  3. If you don't have a rod or skewer, you can twist the strips by hand to form the rotini shape, though it might take a bit of practice to get the perfect shape.
